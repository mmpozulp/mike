About Mike!

My name is Mike Pozulp. My last name is supposed to be Lithuanian, but is most likely a recently-made-up derivative of some Lithuanian name (probably with twice the consonants and half the vowels of "Pozulp") that was discontinued for business reasons. Even though I'm 50% Lithuanian, I am at least a fifth- or sixth-generation American, enough that I can't speak a word of it or even find it on a map. But I am from Chicago, historicallly an eastern europerean immigrant hub, and my uncle owns a meatpacking company, historically an industry centralized in Chicago which typically had labor supplied by eastern european men. If you're interested, The Jungle by Upton Sinclair is a good primary source from the very early 20th century. I still need to read it.

I am a senior at the College of William & Mary in Williamsburg, Virginia. At school I work for our High Performance Computing Group and I was also a resident assistant. I have had the good fortune to intern at two different NASA Centers, Ames and Langley. I had a better time at Ames, but I felt more at home at Langley. In the former I was doing high-performance computing work research. In the latter I was doing autonomous robotics work. Returning from my NASA experience I have brand new interests in aeronautics, climate science, physics and astronomy.

I have a lot of non-professional interests. Enough that I need to write them down so that when I have sufficient free time I know what I need to do to keep busy. Juggling, cooking, reading, documentary, botany, programming, wikipedia, straight-razor shaving, running, basketball, music, weight lifting, dogs, disc golf, ultimate frisbee, museums, online courses, women.
